However, realizing a stable, traversable wormhole requires extreme conditions—typically involving exotic negative energy. These equations describe how mass and energy curve space–time. In certain solutions, such as those featuring wormholes, extreme curvatures connect separate points in space–time.
- Einstein–Rosen Bridge (Wormhole):
A wormhole is conceived as a “bridge” with two mouths (or endpoints) connected by a throat. Traditional models require exotic matter with negative energy density to keep the throat open, preventing gravitational collapse.
